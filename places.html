Famous Places in Varanasi

Ganga Ghats

Varanasi is known for its mesmerizing and historic ghats, including Dashashwamedh Ghat, Assi Ghat, and Manikarnika Ghat. These sacred sites attract pilgrims and tourists alike, offering breathtaking views of the sunrise and sunset over the Ganges. The evening Ganga Aarti at Dashashwamedh Ghat is a soul-stirring experience, where priests perform rituals with fire lamps, accompanied by chanting and devotional music.

Temples

Varanasi is home to some of the most revered temples in India, including Kashi Vishwanath Temple, Sankat Mochan Temple, and Durga Temple. The Kashi Vishwanath Temple, dedicated to Lord Shiva, is one of the twelve Jyotirlingas and is visited by millions of devotees every year. Sankat Mochan Temple is renowned for its connection to Lord Hanuman, where thousands come to seek his blessings, while the Durga Temple, with its striking red facade, is dedicated to Goddess Durga.

Sarnath

Sarnath, located just outside Varanasi, is one of the most significant Buddhist pilgrimage sites in the world. It is where Lord Buddha delivered his first sermon after attaining enlightenment. The site features the famous Dhamek Stupa, the ruins of ancient monasteries, and the Ashokan Pillar, symbolizing the rich Buddhist heritage. The Sarnath Museum houses an impressive collection of Buddhist artifacts and sculptures, making it a must-visit destination for history enthusiasts.

Ramnagar Fort

Ramnagar Fort, situated on the eastern bank of the Ganges, is an architectural marvel built in the 18th century by the kings of Varanasi. The fort showcases a blend of Mughal and Indian architecture, featuring grand courtyards, ornate balconies, and intricate carvings. Inside, the museum displays a fascinating collection of royal artifacts, vintage cars, medieval weapons, and antique manuscripts. The fort is also famous for hosting the grand Ramlila festival, which attracts thousands of spectators.

Banaras Hindu University

Founded in 1916 by Pandit Madan Mohan Malaviya, Banaras Hindu University (BHU) is one of India's most prestigious educational institutions. Spread over 1,300 acres, the university boasts lush greenery, grand colonial-style buildings, and a serene atmosphere. The campus houses the iconic Bharat Kala Bhavan Museum, which features an extensive collection of paintings, sculptures, and historical artifacts. The Vishwanath Temple within the campus is a modern replica of the original Kashi Vishwanath Temple and serves as a spiritual hub for students and visitors alike.
